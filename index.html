Two dice, always. Every turn rolls both dice together.
Doubles = double sixes. Any matching pair (1‑1 … 6‑6) behaves exactly like rolling 6 & 6 — it opens a token even without a real six, moves 6 spaces per die, and grants another roll.
First‑turn mercy roll. The very first time you roll and can't open a token (no six, no double), you get exactly one bonus reroll — once per player, for the whole game.
Kill bonus. Capture an opponent's token → instant extra roll.
Home bonus. Walk a token all the way home → instant extra roll.
Blood before home. None of your tokens may enter the home column until you've captured at least one opponent token during your current life cycle. Once you land a kill, all four of your tokens are cleared to come home. If every one of your tokens is later sent back to base, that clearance is revoked until you kill again.
Bonus rolls stack infinitely — a kill after a double after a double after a home‑run just keeps the same turn going.
